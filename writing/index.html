not quite anything is a mix of science fiction and gender theory. it is written both against essentialist conceptions of gender identity that neglect gender's material basis, and models of gender-as-class that fail to do justice to the way that the subject makes gender legible for themselves. instead, it tries to reckon with gender as an embodied transcendence, a material commitment and inescapable situation but also a possibility for radical autonomy.
